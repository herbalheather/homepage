    <div class="safety">
        <h2>Detailed Foraging Safety Tips for Beginners (US Backyard Herbs)</h2>
        <p>Before swapping in wild finds, follow these 2025-updated rules from expert guides to avoid risks like mis-ID or contamination. Focus: Sustainable, family-safe picks.</p>
        <ul>
            <li><strong>Positive Identification First:</strong> Use 2+ sources (e.g., apps like PictureThis or iNaturalist, field guides) to confirm 100%. Many edibles have toxic look-alikes (e.g., wild carrot vs. poison hemlock—fatal if wrong).</li>
            <li><strong>Avoid Contaminated Spots:</strong> Stay 50ft from roads, farms (pesticides), pet areas, or polluted water. Test soil if possible; urban backyards need extra caution for heavy metals.</li>
            <li><strong>If in Doubt, Leave It Out:</strong> Never taste/test unknown plants—start with "sit spot" observation. For kids/teens: Supervise hunts; teach "no touch" rule for unfamiliar greens.</li>
            <li><strong>Sustainable Harvest:</strong> Take no more than 1/3 of a patch; leave roots intact unless needed. Assess population—skip rare/endangered (e.g., check USDA lists for your state).</li>
            <li><strong>Respect Laws & Ethics:</strong> Get permission on private land; follow public park rules (e.g., no digging in national forests). Wash all forages thoroughly; dry/store away from light.</li>
            <li><strong>Health Checks:</strong> Start small doses; note allergies. Pregnant/nursing? Avoid strong herbs like nettle without doc OK. For pets: Vet-vet everything first.</li>
            <li><strong>Seasonal Smarts (Fall 2025):</strong> Oct is prime for roots/berries—wear gloves for irritants (e.g., burdock). Join local groups (e.g., via Meetup) for guided walks.</li>
        </ul>
        <p><em>Pro Tip:</em> Build a family "Forage Journal" for IDs/photos—ties into quests!</p>
    </div>
